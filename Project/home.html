Oases of warmth
Our unique sauna world

Sauna bathing is a millennia-old tradition that benefits both the body and soul. It promotes blood circulation, detoxifies, and strengthens the immune system. It's not just about the warmth, but also the experience and the ambience. In our sauna landscape, we offer a variety of different saunas to create exactly this experience in many different ways.

  • Heaven&Hell: Heaven&Hell truly turns the sauna world upside down. In this unique spa area, two saunas await you with a magnificent panoramic view.
  • Log cabin sauna: Here, an authentic sauna experience awaits you in traditional wooden construction. The rustic ambience and the scent of wood combine to create a soothing relaxation experience.
  • Dolomite cave steam bath: Immerse yourself in a mystical atmosphere. The steam bath, inspired by the Dolomites, offers relaxation as well as beneficial care for your skin.
  • Herbal sauna: Experience the healing powers of nature in the herbal sauna. Wrapped in the soothing scents of selected herbs, you'll feel stress and tension melt away.
  • Panoramic pasture sauna: Here you will not only enjoy the pleasant warmth, but also a breathtaking view of the surrounding mountains. It is as if you were taking a sauna on an alpine pasture, surrounded by the beauty of nature.
  • Steam bath: A classic among sauna offerings. The steam bath opens the pores, cleanses, and revitalizes the skin while the muscles relax.
  • Infrared sauna: The gentle warmth of infrared rays enters deeply into the skin, boosting blood circulation and relieving muscle tension. A particularly smooth sauna experience that is also ideally suited for sauna beginners.

Discover the diversity of our sauna offerings and find your personal break from everyday life. A unique wellness experience awaits you!🤗

Hubertus History
... from a guesthouse to an active and wellness resort

Did you know that the Hotel Hubertus already exists since 1984? At that time, it was a small guesthouse with only a few rooms. In order to increase the occupancy rate, the Gasser family founded two travel agencies for bus tourism in 1992. Gradually, renovations and extensions were carried out. Already in 1994, the first investment in sustainability was made and a wood-chip heating system was built. An absolute highlight of the hotel is the Skypool, which was completed in 2016. However, this amazing construction was not the end of the expansion work. It was followed in 2022 by the construction of the free-floating wellness platform Heaven&Hell with its relaxation room. A unique architectural masterpiece.

In the mood for a slice of pizza?
Follow the delicious smell towards the Hubertus oven!

Baking pizza like a professional? It's easy here at the Alpin Panorama Hotel Hubertus. Just come to the Hubertus oven and learn from pizzaiolo Paolo Monaco how to prepare traditional Italian pizzas. 🍕

To make sure that you can use the skills you've learned also at home, here's the recipe:

Ingredients:

900 g      flour "00"
100 g      wholemeal flour
5 g          fresh yeast
30 g        salt (fine)
580 ml    water
30 ml      extra virgin olive oil

Preparation:

  • Dissolve the yeast in the water
  • Mix it with the flour
  • Add salt
  • Add olive oil
  • Leave it to rest for 20 minutes at room temperature
  • Form dough pieces (approx. 250g)
  • Leave the dough to rest at room temperature for 5 hours
  • Roll out the dough
  • Cover it
  • Place the dough into the oven (approx. 20 minutes at 200° - depending on the thickness of the dough)

Enjoy!

Oh, I see, you're not so into cooking? 😏 Don't worry! You are also welcome to just stop by and enjoy a delicious slice of pizza. 😉

Relaxation for body and mind
Forest bathing - "Shinrin Yoku”

"Taking a bath in the atmosphere of the forest". This is how the Japanese health trend Shinrin Yoku can be translated - or simply: forest bathing.

Who doesn't know the revitalizing feeling that comes over you when you breathe in the fresh air during a short walk in the forest and immediately feel much more relaxed and balanced? The forest has an enormous healing potential, because in addition to relaxation, it provides stimulation without being too demanding. In a mindful interaction with the forest, one can rediscover one's natural balance.

Here are three exercise suggestions for you:

1 Feel your roots

Find a comfortable place in the forest, where you can stand safely and steadily. You are also invited to take off your shoes. Close your eyes. Now consciously pay attention to your breath flow and feel how you absorb energy from the forest air with each inhalation and how you let the blockages and stress flow away with each exhalation.

2 Sensory journey

For this exercise, too, first find a comfortable place. Breathe in and out deeply three times and then focus your attention on each sense in turn. What do you see? Look at the forest around you. Then close your eyes. What do you hear? Keep your eyes closed and then try to feel the forest atmosphere. What do you feel? Finally, take a few deep breaths. What do you smell?

3 Mindful walking at a snail's pace

Choose a path in the forest and move forward as mindfully as possible. Try to feel every movement - every step, every push and roll of the foot. Does the forest floor feel dry, damp, cold, warm, soft or uneven? What can you feel under your feet?

Make your own bread
Baking like in the past

All of us know that there is nothing as delicious as fresh bread for breakfast. It couldn't be a better start into the day. A good mood is pre-programmed! 🤗

The daily trip to the bakery is no more necessary. You can simply bake your own bread at home! As a part of our weekly programme, we will show you how to shape the bread loaves and bake them in a traditional bread oven. Of course, you can then also taste the homemade bread. 😋

To ensure that the bread is as delicious at home as it is at the Hubertus, here is the recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg rye flour
  • 200 g wheat flour
  • 10 g of yeast
  • ca. 3/4 lt water (lukewarm)
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon Bread clover (gypsy)
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon fennel

Preparation:

Mix the flour and spices, make a pit, add the yeast mixed with tepid water and knead everything into a soft dough. Then leave the dough to rest in a warm place for approx. 1-2 hours, shape it, place it on a floured towel and leave it to rest again for 30 minutes. Then bake the bread at 220 degrees for about 45 minutes.

What a delicious taste! 😍

In our immediate vicinity there are countless beautiful winter hiking trails that take you into the solitude of the mountains. One of our absolute favourites is the legendardy Fanes Alm in the heart of the Dolomites. It almost seems as if the bizarre peaks, which tower far into the deep blue winter sky, are a trellis for the winter hikers. The impressive path winds its way from Pederü up to the Fanes Alm, where you can stop for a bite even in the coldest of seasons, before you make another detour to the Fanes Pass. The view that you get enjoy from up there … it fascinates even us South Tyroleans again and again.

We will be happy to tell you more insider tips for winter hiking in person.
